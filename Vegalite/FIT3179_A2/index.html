World Income, Education and Health

World Economics often contains useful information for data analysis. Income
and GDP are the most interesting topics of all time.
In 1978, the first World Development Report introduced groupings
of low income and middle income countries.

In the 1983 WDR, the Middle Income Group was split into
lower middle and upper middle groups and in 1989,
a high income country definition was introduced.

Income Classifications

Here is a map that shows the Income Classifications for countries.
Zoom / Filter by Continent feature is allowed for this map.

We can see that many countries have increased their income
through the years.

Human Development Index

Now we know that World Income has been increasing as green colour increases, we can see how the world has developed through years.

We use a measure called the Human Development Index. The HDI is a summary composite measure of a country's average achievements in three basic aspects of human development:
Health, Knowledge and Standard of living.

The slope graph on the right shows the change of the HDI values of each continent. Note how all of them increases.

The legend can be clicked to select a specific continent.

Out of the 3 aspects mentioned above, I will include visualisations for two aspects: Education and Health.

Education

We all know that knowledge can change lives. This bar chart
below shows the changes in the Government Expenditure
on Tertiary Education by Continent in different periods.

Tertiary Education Expenditure

The bar chart on the left shows 3 different periods: Before 2005, Between 2005 - 2010, and After 2010. Legends can be selected to see a specific period.

Notice how the latest period (After 2010) usually has the highest value. From this graph, we can say that governments are spending more of country income on education of the people.

Now we know that the government are spending more money on tertiary education, we can see how it affects total education rate. The map below shows the Enrollment Ratio in Tertiary Education.

Take note how the light yellow part decreases and purple gets darker through the years. You can zoom in to Selected Continents for a closer view.

From the map above we see how more people are receiving higher level education. Now, let's see how education helps in development of the world.

The graph below is a Population Pyramid that shows the Employability Rate of men and women, before and after 2000. These two periods were chosen as from the map above, we see the significant increase in enrolment rate after 2000

Population Employability Rate by Gender

Not to our surprise, we can see from the lables, that the women employability rate has increased in almost all continents!

This is because, as income increases, people's lives improves, and as more people get educated, the toxic phenomenon - Gender Inequality that was once global, is decreasing.

This is very exciting news and it just shows how Education is so important to us as human beings.

Health

After analysing how income affects education around the world,
now we will dive into how the world has developed through the years in terms of health.

Here is a map that shows the Total healthcare expenditure
as the share of national gross domestic product (GDP) for countries.

Note how the green gets darker averagely in most areas of the world.
Zoom / Filter by Continent is also allowed.

It is a good thing now that Governments are spending more on Healthcare and people are being protected.

This is good because governments and people now know the importance of health, and it is a lifelong investment.

The two graphs below show how the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) of a Continent directly affects the Life Expectancy and another crucial factor to determine the development of a country: Child Mortality Rate.

Child Mortality Rate by Continents

It is good to see that the Child Mortality Rate has decreased in all continents, especially Africa.

Another interesting observation is how Health Expenditure affects Child Mortality Rate.

Note how the width of the lines shows the relationship: As Health Expenditure increases, Child Mortality Rate decreases.

It is available to select by Continents from the legend, to see the line for a specific continent.


Life Expectancy

Finally, our final graph shows the relationship between Life Expectancy and Gross Domestic Product (GDP) throughout the years.

If we alide through the years, we can see that Life Expextancy and GDP per capita is constantly positively correlated.

I think this result is expected, the reason is explained as: As income increases, education rate increases, people gain more awareness for health, and take care of their health better. Hence, Life Expectancy increases.

Other than that, we can also see how the cluster of bubbles are moving towards the right as a whole. This shows the same result as our other graphs above about the increase in World Income, and the improvement in Health.
